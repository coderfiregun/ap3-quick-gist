# Low Level System Design (LLD) Course

## Course Snapshot
- **Format:** 5 weeks, 3 instructor-led sessions per week, 2 hours per session
- **Support:** Post-class doubt resolution, community access, and interview prep clinics
- **Access:** Lifetime recordings for all batches across both C++ and Java tracks
- **Resources:** Comprehensive code samples, notes, and quizzes for each module

## Weekly Roadmap

### Week 1 – Foundations & Principles
- OOP refresher and core design heuristics (DRY, KISS, YAGNI)
- Deep dive into SOLID principles with practical refactoring exercises
- Reading and drafting UML class diagrams for requirement capture
- Creational patterns: factory method and abstract factory in practice

### Week 2 – Creational & Behavioral Patterns
- Singleton lifecycle management and thread-safe implementations
- Builder, prototype, and object cloning strategies
- Observer, command, and chain of responsibility for event-driven flows
- Iterators and collection traversal abstractions

### Week 3 – Behavioral & Structural Patterns
- Strategy and template method for interchangeable behaviors
- Adapter and decorator for API compatibility and feature extensions
- Bridge, composite, proxy, and facade for modular interfaces
- Anti-pattern awareness and pattern selection checklists

### Week 4 – Applied System Designs
- Requirement analysis workshops and documenting assumptions
- Case studies: IRCTC reservation, chess engine, elevator controller
- Translating specs into class diagrams, sequence diagrams, and code skeletons
- Emphasis on extensibility, testability, and error handling

### Week 5 – Capstones & Interview Prep
- Recommendation engine design walkthrough
- Collaborative meeting scheduler implementation
- Mock interviews, whiteboard strategies, and storytelling frameworks
- Revision sprints and question banks for last-mile preparation

## Deliverables & Resources
- Language-specific starter templates (C++ and Java)
- Pattern cheat sheets and UML stencils
- Problem statement repository with sample solutions
- Lifetime updates, alumni mock interviews, and peer review circles
